Bichon Frise: temperament, care and good with kids

Updated July 4, 2026 · dog breed guide

The Bichon Frise is a cheerful, friendly little dog with a curly white coat that barely sheds — so it's often suitable for allergy sufferers. It's a cuddly apartment companion.

Temperament

It's playful, sociable and affectionate. It loves attention and copes poorly with being alone. It tends to be spirited and trainable, and enjoys showing off tricks.

Good with children

★★★★

It suits children well thanks to its cheerful, tolerant nature. Because of its small size, supervise play with the youngest so they don't accidentally hurt it.

Care & upkeep

The curly coat doesn't shed but needs regular clipping (every 4–8 weeks) and frequent brushing against matting. Tear stains form around the eyes and need cleaning.

Exercise & daily routine

Short walks and play at home are enough, around 30–60 minutes a day. It's a low-maintenance apartment breed suitable even for less active households.
