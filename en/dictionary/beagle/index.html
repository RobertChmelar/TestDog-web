Beagle: temperament, care and good with kids

Updated July 4, 2026 · dog breed guide

The Beagle is a cheerful, friendly scent hound with a tireless nose. It's hardy, sociable and kind to children — while also being headstrong and driven by smells.

Temperament

The Beagle is an optimist and a companion. It gets on great with people and dogs and copes poorly with being alone. Its weakness is its nose: once it catches a scent, it happily forgets commands.

Good with children

★★★★

It suits children well — playful, sturdy and friendly. Being medium-sized, it's neither fragile nor dangerously large. It needs consistency so it doesn't steal food.

Care & upkeep

The short coat is low-maintenance — brush once a week. Check the long ears (prone to infections). The Beagle loves food and gains weight easily, so watch portions.

Exercise & daily routine

It needs lots of exercise and scent games — ideally over an hour a day. Because of its hunting drive, only let it off-lead in safe places, otherwise it follows a scent.

Who the Beagle suits

Suitable for active families that can handle consistent training and lots of exercise. Not suited to homes where the dog is alone for long or where occasional 'baying' is a problem.
