Pug: temperament, care and good with kids

Updated July 4, 2026 · dog breed guide

The Pug is an easygoing, affectionate little companion with an unmistakable face. It dislikes hard exertion and is a classic apartment lap dog — with a few health specifics.

Temperament

It's friendly, cuddly and calm. It loves being close to people and copes poorly with being alone. It's usually cheerful and non-confrontational, welcoming to children and strangers.

Good with children

★★★★

It suits children well thanks to its mild, cuddly nature. Because of its small size and sensitive breathing, make sure children don't overtax it or leave it in the heat.

Care & upkeep

The short coat sheds more than you'd expect — brush once a week. Cleaning the facial skin folds and watching weight are essential. As a brachycephalic breed the Pug breathes with more effort.

Exercise & daily routine

Short walks are enough, around 30–45 minutes a day. It doesn't tolerate heat or strain — in summer walk only in the cool and never leave it in a car.

Who the Pug suits

Ideal for families, seniors and individuals in apartments who want a calm lap dog. Not suited to sporty owners or a hot environment without a way to cool down.
