Siberian Husky: temperament, care and good with kids

Updated July 4, 2026 · dog breed guide

The Siberian Husky is a beautiful, friendly and independent sled dog with a huge need for exercise and a tendency to escape. It's a breed for experienced, active owners.

Temperament

It's friendly to everyone, including strangers — so it's useless as a guard dog. It's independent, stubborn and driven by the instinct to run. It loves its pack and copes poorly with being alone.

Good with children

★★★

It's friendly and tolerant with children, but its energy and independence require supervision and experienced handling — hence three stars. It's not an obedient on-command dog.

Care & upkeep

The dense double coat sheds massively, especially twice a year — brush several times a week. Otherwise the Husky is a healthy, clean breed without the typical doggy smell.

Exercise & daily routine

It needs an enormous amount of exercise — running, sled sports, long hikes, easily 2+ hours a day. Because of their urge to run, only let them off-leash in safe places and secure the yard.

Who the Siberian Husky suits

Suitable for experienced, very active owners and athletes, ideally with a second dog. Definitely not for beginners, a quiet apartment life or a hot climate.
