Border Collie: temperament, care and good with kids

Updated July 4, 2026 · dog breed guide

The Border Collie is considered the smartest dog breed. It's incredibly trainable and hard-working — and has a huge need for exercise and tasks that makes it a dog for active people only.

Temperament

It's sharp, focused and forever ready to work. Without a job it gets bored and finds its own — often unwanted — activity. It tends to be sensitive and strongly bonded to its person.

Good with children

★★★

It can be great with children, but its herding instinct leads it to 'gather' them — circling and nipping at heels. It needs training and supervision, hence three stars.

Care & upkeep

The medium to semi-long coat needs brushing once or twice a week. The demands are in the head, not the coat — mental work is key.

Exercise & daily routine

It needs at least two hours of active exercise plus tasks or a dog sport (agility, flyball). Without it, it suffers and becomes problematic.
